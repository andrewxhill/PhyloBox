PhyloBox is a tool to allow researchers to go from phylogeny to publication quality images and web publishable widgets in a minimum number of steps. We plan to support many of the same tools as Mesquite.

From PhyloBox a researcher can edit the form and look of a phylogenetic tree as well as share control of that tree with collaborators. Meanwhile, storage of that tree is simple and versioning new trees is a breeze.

Using the URI elements within PhyloXML we plan to support rich integration of video, audio, and other content into the phylogenetic tree visualizations.

We wanted to make web publishing a snap, so we have designed a widget (similar to YouTube or SlideShare) that you can drop into your webpage with minimum hastle and allow your readers can explore the tree, including in full-screen mode.

PhyloBox is the work of Andrew Hill, Sander Pick and Rob Guralnick and was funded in part by grants from the NSF and the University of Colorado Natural History Museum.
